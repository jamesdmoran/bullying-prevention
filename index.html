An ambitious non-profit to help eradicate bullying

Group therapy is an excellent way to help chronic bullying victims, but represents less than 2% of psychotherapy services.

Why isn't there more group therapy?

  1. Population size: Sourcing groups of similar aged students available at the same time requires a pool of participants larger than most communities
  2. Stigma: Many victims don't report bullying out of fear of retaliation
  3. Complexity: Groups require marketing and admin beyond the capabilities of most practices

Our approach solves all three problems at once:

  • Zoom-based, counselor-led cohorts of students of similar ages and interests
  • Nationwide reach: Participants from across the country, with none attending the same school
  • Structured curriculum: Six sessions focused on demystifying bullying, developing social, problem solving and coping skills. And an additional session for parents
  • Trusted referral channel: A pathway for schools to refer families experiencing bullying
  • Full support for providers: We handle all marketing, coordination, scheduling, insurance and technology so counselors can simply login and provide care

We've already proven this works:

We have been successfully piloting the initiative for over a year in a clinical setting (University of Nebraska - Brave Lab) and have developed a detailed curriculum that's driving meaningful results.
