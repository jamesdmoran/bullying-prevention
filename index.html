Outside

An ambitious non-profit for bullying prevention

Outside helps chronic bullying victims through counselor-led, Zoom-based group therapy cohorts. We’re hiring a Dallas-based Director to launch and scale the program nationwide.

Group therapy is an evidence-based bullying intervention - an ideal setting to build social, problem-solving, and coping skills - yet it accounts for less than 2 % of psychotherapy services.

Why isn't there more group therapy?

  1. Population size: Sourcing groups of similar aged students available at the same time requires a pool of participants larger than most communities
  2. Stigma: Many victims don't report bullying out of fear of retaliation
  3. Complexity: Groups require marketing and admin beyond the capabilities of most practices

Our approach solves all three problems at once:

  • Zoom-based, counselor-led cohorts of students of similar ages and interests
  • Privacy-first: cohorts are drawn from across the country, no two attendees attend the same school
  • Evidence-based curriculum: Six sessions focused on demystifying bullying, developing social, problem solving and coping skills. With an additional session for parents
  • Trusted referral channel: A pathway for schools to refer families experiencing bullying
  • Easy for providers: We handle all marketing, coordination, scheduling, insurance and technology so counselors can simply login and provide care

We've already proven this works:

This initiative has been successfully piloted for over a year in a clinical setting - Bullying Research and Victim Empowerment (BRAVE) Lab - with a curriculum that's driving meaningful results.

Research

Our approach is grounded in research on bullying victimization, mental health impacts, and effective interventions.

Research underway

Bullying, Empowerment, and Learning Online Network Group (BELONG)

BRAVE Lab, University of Nebraska - Lincoln. Program details

  • Free online group program led by trained facilitators for middle school students (grades 6-8) who have experienced bullying.
  • Six-week weekly Zoom sessions with a facilitator and 4-5 same-age, same-gender peers.
  • Focuses on identifying bullying, building problem-solving and coping skills, and strengthening peer connections.

Research papers

Empirical and theoretical foundations of family interventions to reduce the incidence and mental health impacts of school bullying victimization

Karyn L. Healy, Hannah J. Thomas, Matthew R. Sanders & James G. Scott (2022)
International Review of Psychiatry, 34:2, 140-153. DOI

  • "There has been substantial longitudinal research on the relationship between the ability to regulate emotions and bullying victimisation: internalising problems are both risk factors and consequences of bullying."
  • "Emotional reactivity or dysregulation has been found to be the single most important predictor in the emergence of chronic victimisation."
  • "Supportive relationships with both peers and family are protective against bullying victimisation and the mental health consequences of being bullied."

Examining the Effectiveness of School-Bullying Intervention Programs Globally: a Meta-analysis
