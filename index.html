Virtual group therapy for bullying victims

Group therapy is an excellent way to help chronic bullying victims, but represents less than 2% of psychotherapy services.

So why isn't there more group therapy?

  1. Population size: Sourcing groups of similar aged students available at the same time requires a pool of participants larger than most communities
  2. Stigma: Many victims don't report bullying out of fear of retalation
  3. Complexity: Groups require marketing and admin beyond the capabilities of most practices

  • Zoom-based, counselor-led cohorts of students of similar ages and interests
  • Statewide reach: Participants are located across the state. No participants attend the same school
  • Structured Curriculum: Six sessions focused on demystifying bullying, problem solving and coping skills, with an additional session for parents
  • Trusted Referral Channel: A pathway for school counselors to refer families experiencing bullying
  • Full Support: We handle all marketing, coordination, scheduling, insurance and technology so counselors can simply login, provide care, and earn a higher rate than they would with individual sessions

We've already proven this works:

We have been successfully piloting the initiative for over a year in a clinical setting (University of Nebraska - Brave Lab) and have developed a detailed curriculum that's driving meaningful results.

Founding Director

Location: Dallas, Texas

Group therapy is an excellent way to help chronic bullying victims, but represents less than 2% of psychotherapy services. We're launching an ambitious non-profit to change that.

Our model removes the three biggest barriers to group care: sourcing a large enough pool of similar-aged participants, reducing stigma, and handling the operational complexity that most practices cannot absorb.

We deliver Zoom-based, counselor-led cohorts with statewide reach (so participants never attend with peers from their own school), a structured six-session curriculum plus a parent session, and a trusted referral pathway for school counselors. We also handle marketing, coordination, scheduling, insurance, and technology so counselors can focus on care.

This is a fully funded non-profit by a Dallas family passionate about the bullying prevention cause.

We have been successfully piloting the initiative for over a year in a clinical setting (University of Nebraska - Brave Lab) and have developed a detailed curriculum that is driving meaningful results.

Your Mandate

We are hiring a Dallas-based Director to launch and lead this initiative. Your primary responsibilities:
